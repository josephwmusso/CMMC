  spreadsheet exported from Google Forms response data. It documents
  completion status for Meridian Aerospace's January 2026 annual security

  covering topics such as password hygiene, recognizing suspicious emails,
  physical security basics, and safe web browsing. The video was hosted on
  YouTube (unlisted link) and employees were directed to watch it and then
  fill out a Google Form confirming completion. The Google Form collected:
  employee name, email address, timestamp of submission, and a single
  yes/no acknowledgment question.

  The spreadsheet shows 12 of 14 employees have completed the training.
  Completion timestamps range from January 5 through January 28, 2026.
  The two employees who have NOT completed the training are Tommy Reeves
  (machinist, shop floor) and Marcus Chen (machinist, shop floor). No
  follow-up or escalation action is documented for the two incomplete
  employees.

  The training content was not tailored to Meridian's specific environment,
  CUI handling responsibilities, or CMMC obligations. It was a generic
  off-the-shelf awareness video with no customization.

  DELIBERATELY ABSENT: There is no phishing simulation program — no
  simulated phishing emails have ever been sent to employees and no
  click-rate metrics exist. There is no role-based training for users with
  elevated privileges (Jason Bell as admin) or for users who handle CUI.
  There is no CUI-specific or CMMC-specific training module. There are no
  quiz scores or knowledge-assessment results — the Google Form only
  confirms the employee watched the video. There is no training plan or
  schedule for remediation of the two non-completions. Prior year training
  records (before 2026) do not exist in digital form.
