content_summary: |
  The Acceptable Use Policy is a 3-page PDF that governs employee use of
  Meridian Aerospace's information systems. It was updated in January 2025
  and is stored as a signed PDF in the company's Google Drive shared folder
  "HR > Policies."

  Page 1 states the purpose and scope, covering all employees and any
  contractors who access company systems. It specifies that Google Workspace
  (Gmail, Google Drive, Google Docs) is the authorized platform for
  business communications and file storage. Personal email services are
  prohibited for business use.

  Page 2 addresses portable media and removable storage. The policy states
  that USB flash drives should only be used for transferring files to shop
  floor CNC machines when network transfer is not available, and that
  employees should not copy sensitive files to personal USB devices.
  However, this restriction is aspirational only — the document
  acknowledges no technical enforcement mechanism is in place, and there
  is no USB device management, endpoint DLP, or port-blocking solution
  deployed. The policy does not define what constitutes "sensitive files."

  Page 3 contains acknowledgment signature lines. Signed copies for all 14
  employees are stored as individual PDFs in Google Drive under
  "HR > Policies > AUP Signed." The signatures confirm employees have read
  and agree to abide by the policy.

  DELIBERATELY ABSENT: The policy does not reference CUI or ITAR-controlled
  data. There is no classification or labeling scheme for data types. There
  is no mention of mobile device management, no policy on personal device
  (BYOD) use, no restrictions on cloud storage outside Google Drive, and
  no defined consequences for policy violations beyond general reference to
  the employee handbook's disciplinary section. There is no mention of
  monitoring or auditing of user activity on Google Workspace.
