content_summary: |
  The Meridian Aerospace Employee Handbook is a 14-page PDF serving as the
  company's primary onboarding and personnel policy document. It was last
  revised in March 2024 and bears the signature acknowledgment pages of all
  14 current employees.

  Section 1 (pages 1-3) covers company history, mission statement, and
  organizational chart listing Jason Bell as owner/general manager.

  Section 2 (pages 4-6) establishes general workplace conduct expectations
  including attendance, dress code, and professional behavior standards for
  the machine shop and office areas.

  Section 3 (pages 7-8) contains a brief Acceptable Use of Technology
  paragraph stating employees must use company-provided Google Workspace
  accounts for business email and must not install unauthorized software.
  This section is high-level and references no specific enforcement
  mechanism or monitoring tools.

  Section 4 (pages 9-10) covers anti-harassment and equal employment
  opportunity policies.

  Section 5 (pages 11-12) describes basic disciplinary procedures and
  termination processes, including return of company property (laptops, USB
  drives, badges). There is no formal offboarding checklist that addresses
  revocation of system access or Google Workspace account deprovisioning
  timelines.

  Section 6 (pages 13-14) is the signature acknowledgment section with
  scanned wet signatures from all 14 employees confirming receipt and
  understanding.

  DELIBERATELY ABSENT: The handbook contains no language about Controlled
  Unclassified Information (CUI), no CUI handling or marking procedures,
  no reference to DFARS or CMMC obligations, no formal incident reporting
  workflow beyond the phrase "report concerns to the owner," no defined
  roles or responsibilities for cybersecurity, and no mention of security
  awareness training requirements. There is no separation of duties policy
  and no background-check requirement documented.
