content_summary: |
  The Incident Response Procedures document is a 2-page Word document that
  serves as Meridian Aerospace's sole incident response guidance. It was
  created in June 2025 by Jason Bell (owner/general manager) who is named
  as the sole incident coordinator.

  Page 1 defines what constitutes a "security incident" in broad terms:
  suspected unauthorized access, malware infection, lost or stolen devices,
  or unusual system behavior. The reporting instruction is for any employee
  who suspects an incident to immediately notify Jason Bell via phone call
  or in person. Jason Bell's personal cell number is listed as the sole
  point of contact. There is no secondary contact, no escalation path, and
  no after-hours coverage plan.

  Page 2 describes three high-level response steps: (1) disconnect the
  affected machine from the network, (2) document what happened in a Google
  Doc, and (3) contact the SonicWall support line if the firewall is
  involved. There is a brief note about restoring from USB backups stored
  in the office safe if data is lost.

  DELIBERATELY ABSENT: The document contains no tabletop exercise history
  and no schedule for future exercises. There is no external notification
  procedure — critically, the DFARS 252.204-7012 requirement to report
  cyber incidents to the DoD within 72 hours is not referenced or
  addressed. There are no evidence preservation steps (no forensic imaging
  guidance, no chain-of-custody procedures, no log retention instructions).
  There is no severity classification scheme, no communication plan for
  affected parties, no roles and responsibilities matrix beyond Jason Bell,
  and no lessons-learned or post-incident review process. The document does
  not reference coordination with any external incident response service,
  law enforcement, or legal counsel.
